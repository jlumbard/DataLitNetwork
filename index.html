Mission

The Mission of DataLit Network is to bridge the gap between youth and understanding data to empower them to excel in the data-driven workforce as critical online citizens.

Our goal is to target young, captive audiences and demystify humanityâ€™s complex relationship with data. One day, we hope to see data as a key topic in the formative years of education.

Vision

Connect under-informed communities with access to education on the prizes and perils of data, creating an aware and empowered society.

Why Data Literacy?

General Benefits of Data:

  • Data literacy implemented into education as a stepping stone for a career in the tech industry, or others that involve the use of such technology.
  • Can educate youth on how to work with data analytics in a career setting.
  • When creating and working with the data, one must be aware of how their actions can prove both helpful or harmful to themselves and others.
General Risks:
  • Cybersecurity and privacy risks. Pivotal to understand how data works in the background with each engine search, and click of a button.
  • Predictive analytics and how they can push the ethical boundaries of privacy.
  • Understanding user agreements when downloading apps and logging onto websites and what the conditions entail, how they may be negative for the user.
  • Technology and data are vulnerable to practices of fraud.

About

DataLit was born out of the University of Western Ontario, by students with diverse educational backgrounds in computer sciences, engineering, business, media, and information technology studies.

With a surge in the role of technology in every industry ensues the need to educate youth on both the benefits, risks, and ethical implications of data.
